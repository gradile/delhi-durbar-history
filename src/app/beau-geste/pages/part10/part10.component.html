Chapter 10

There have always been small tendencies within the 1903 Delhi Durbar collectors. There are times that they ask for more palanquins, or more music bands, and even for more camels. 2004 was a year for palanquins. After Jaisalmer's and Alwar's we thought it was time for a new one. We found photos of a particularly elegant one within Rewa's group at the Retainers Review. It was one of the 12 silver palanquins they brought.

Daniel loved it and we went for it.

Beau Geste Set 126 – Rewa retainers escorting (one of the dozen) silver state litter(s), Delhi Durbar 1903.
(crazy, but that's how we called it )

It was 2003 when we came across the Corbis photo. So far, we had only seen Wheeler's book photographs from an original edition, and it was the first peek to a high definition as an experience. When we found the Sikkim elephant photo online we decided to buy the digital copy. We were absolutely amazed when we received the cd.
The detail in the photo was fascinating. Now you can buy digital copies that come in huge files through online file transfer websites. But then, the only way to do that was to receive your files by mail in a cd.

This was the whole photo, and the details that could bear the enlargement. The final result depends on the amount of dpi with which they scan the original photos, but basically, they depend on the original negative. If it was in great focus it was a blessing. And the Sikkim Elephant photo was.

Beau Geste Set 142 – The State Elephant of Sikkim, Delhi Durbar, 1903.

At that moment and for some reason I took the picture with the guards in it, and never found the right moment to retake the photo with the elephant alone. My bad. The photo had allowed us already to make the Sikkim guards. But we could also see clearly the rest of the figures going with it.

Beau Geste Set 143 – Sikkim's Elephant Guards Escort, Delhi Durbar 1903.

The following set was a pair of sets in fact. We fell in love with the Ladakhi Devil Dancers that Gertrude Bell had taken in her photograph.

With the years we got many good pictures of them but at the moment, we could research about them directly. They still dance with the same clothes and masks, so the more recent online photos were very very valuable for us. We made them with the monks that played the music as well.

The Ladakhi Devil Dancers set has been the best seller set of Beau Geste. Of ALL Beau Geste. WWI, and French Foreign Legion collectors also bought them. Even Napoleonic people . They are stunning

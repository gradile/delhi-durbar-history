Chapter 12

Back to the Beau Geste Delhi Durbar sets history chapters. But first, I wanted to show a few photos of one of the sets I've already featured in the last chapter. Every now and then we receive requests for the elephants to be painted in matte finish. We consider those special commissions, and we produce them for the collector. It was the case of the State Elephant of Alwar, set #190. I wanted to share with you a few pictures of the set.

In case you are wondering, we only offer the matte finished paintings as a special commission and not as a standard set. Some collectors don't like glossy so they choose this option, but the vast majority go for the glossy finish. I believe the shining that glossy colors shows can't be compared with anything else, specially for the Delhi Durbar sets. But I am aware of the charm that matte paint brings. It's mainly a matter of taste. And we can make both.

Next came another palanquin. A collector took photos during his trip to India and sent images of the Jhodpur Museum. He wanted a particular palanquin made. It was absolutely beautiful. So we went for it, and made a woman siting on it because it looked feminine to us. We never knew for sure who rode in it.

Beau Geste Delhi Durbar set 192 - Palanquin of Jhodpur, Delhi Durbar 1903.

I really go through the Delhi Durbar photos that I got to have during the years a lot. And I mean A LOT. Going through the stereoview scans that my friend Bill kindly shared with me, I found the palanquin in one of them. This is a portrait of the Indore group. I already made a few sets portraying these men. But looking closely (VERY closely), I found the palanquin in the background! I am not sure if it is the same one, even though it looks very similar. I don't even know if it belongs to Indore or if the Jhodpur group happened to be resting beside the Indore group. It was an open field and people grouped by state, but were free to go anywhere. It is interesting to see the definition that a small stereoview can have.

---------------------------------------------------------

The following State Elephant was the Travancore State one. This time we had better photos, thanks to the digital copies bought from the British Library. But as the Travancore elephant came in second place on the right column, right after the State Elephant of Hyderabad, it was in almost all of the photographs of the beginning of the procession. So this time we could be accurate with the details, more like we prefer our work to be.

Beau Geste set 200 – State Elephant of Travancore, Delhi Durbar 1903.

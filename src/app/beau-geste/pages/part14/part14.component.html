Chapter 14

I had never had to go through my photos as thoroughly as I have been doing lately due to the research for each chapter. And I found photos that I didn't remember I had. I found 3 pictures of the masters of the Jhodpur palanquin. I thought you'd like to see them. Also, I found the first picture that we took of the Beau Geste staff. Just a few of us. Our staff grew and decreased through the years. Always the best people ever.

The very first Beau Geste staff

---------------------------------------------------------

Going back to the sets. It was a time where we were hungry for photos of the Delhi Durbar. We wanted to add new sets to the line. Anything from India from that period was useful to us, since it is certain to assume that everybody was brought to the Delhi for the event. I found a (very bad) photocopy in the cabinets at the British Library. It was a group from Kashmir. The hats were amazing so we decided to make them. I found another photo of these guys later, researching about Kashmir online.

Beau Geste Set 216 - Kashmir Infantry, Jammu and Kashmir, Delhi Durbar 1903.

Beau Geste Set 216 - Kashmir Military Band, Jammu and Kashmir, Delhi Durbar 1903

---------------------------------------------------------

By then, the akali had been a great success. I found one of them, mounted on a pony, in one of Gertrude Bell's photos. Reviewing Menpes plates I found the color plate. We decided to make a couple of mounted akali, one set for each state from which we had already released the walking ones. They are a lovely lovely set. I like akali very much. They are warriors with such personality.

Beau Geste Set 218A - Akalis on pony from Patiala, Delhi Durbar 1903.

Beau Geste Set 218B - Akalis on pony from Jind, Delhi Durbar 1903.

Years later I bought a wonderful photo from the BL that had 3 akali portrayed: one walking, one on pony, and one on the elephant. I fell in love with the picture. It is a beautiful photo, with much detail. No need to say that, many years later, I ended up making the akali on elephant. All of them from Patiala.

---------------------------------------------------------

As most of you know by now, the music bands had become very popular by then. And the Retainers Review had SO many of them. The journalists and writers mention the music and the bands all the time. They represented a huge part of the event as well.
There are certain parts of the parades that were very much photographed, painted and described. One of those groups were the men on stilts from Cutch. Marlborough had made them already. Luckily for us, most pictures showed them followed by a palanquin and a wonderful band with silver instruments. Instruments that we had never made before, and that added uniqueness to the groups. No need to say how much we enjoyed making those.

Beau Geste Set 229 - Silver Horns' Band from Cutch, Delhi Durbar 1903.

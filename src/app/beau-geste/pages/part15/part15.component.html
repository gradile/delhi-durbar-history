New Delhi Durbar 1903 film.

Spot from where the film was taken.

I left this 1903 Delhi Durbar film for the end, for a number or reasons. It was the first film where I could see the elephants marching and the feeling was inexplicable. I couldn't believe that I was actually seeing it. By that moment I didn't really know what was what I was looking at, specially identifying elephants and other participants. It turned out to be somewhat film or developed reversed. It took me some time to understand that. The elephants are walking along Chandni Chowk and the film was taken somewhere from or around the Futtehpoorie Musjid, just from the corner where the elephants turned towards Ahmad Pai, in their way of leaving the city. If you are familiarized with the elephants, you will see that the column closer to the camera, that looks like the right column, is actually the left one. The confirmation of that is the lining troops. The regiment lining had a band on the left side and the pipers on the right side. Which are reversed in the film. What is also reversed is the order in which the elephants and authorities walked along the street. In the film you will see the Retainers elephants in front (pay attention to the Baby Elephant and Rewa Executioner), when they were at the end of the parade. It is the only film (known) that shows the retainers. After them came the real front of the parade, Imperial Cadets, British authorities, the Elephants of the Aides-de-Camps. Then, the Main Procession, not complete. It begins with Gwalior (not showing the first elephants) and cuts after Narsingharh, to go back to the last 6 elephants at the end of the elephants columns. But the best part of the film is all the other representatives and authorities that were there and haven't been photographed much. The British authorities, the Cavalry, the Heralds!. Even the Balusch chiefs are there! Shame that the image is not neat, but it's very exciting to be able to see them.

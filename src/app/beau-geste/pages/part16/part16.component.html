Chapter 16

At this point of the Durbar line we had released nine elephants, and we were beginning to see the need of adding figures to complete the scene. We had added the lining troops and the spectators by then, but the elephants needed the soldiers, servants and musicians that surrounded them. So the following two sets were two groups of these guys, one for Indore and one for Patiala. The information to make them was right at the same photo that we used for the elephants. So there we went.

They were followed by two new elephants. The majestic state elephant of Indore, and the dazzling executioner from Rewa's elephant. Daniel was very excited with this last one, it was so unique and impressive. He made it with such detail, he could achieve in it everything that the elephant was. We couldn’t be prouder of them both. That September (2009) we brought them both to the Chicago show and they were a complete success. Collectors loved them very much. We were so happy. And then…

Daniel died three months later. Some of you surely remember the shock of hearing about it. My options were simple: to continue or not. When I was able to use my mind again, much later, the decision was clear. HE would have wanted me to keep on working on a project that was all for us, that we loved immensely, and I wanted that too, very much. So from now on, all the sets featuring the history chapters are only made by me. Only me doing the research, only me deciding and only me on the make: sculpture and painting. Many of you were with me in these process, and I will always thank you for being there for me.

My son Julian was away when this happened. But when he returned he helped more and more as years went by. He is my right hand now, so I am not alone in this project any more.

Fortunately, for this guy, there were many photos and a color plate. One of the photos that we had bought from the British Library featured the elephant going around the Jumma Masjid, within the Retainers Procession that followed the Main one.

For some reason that I couldn’t find an answer yet, the yellow in these photos photographed as black. Same happened with the red color. The main proof of this was the first elephant in the procession, the State Elephant of Hyderabad, that was the elephant more described in books and newspapers. It was golden yellow. But his clothes looked black in all the photos.

Detail of the executioner's howda. It looks black at first sight. But looking closely the stripes of the tiger can be seen, black over yellow.

Now, about the new elephant. At this point the elephants that remain unmade from the Main Procession are less photographed. This means that I have to build it from a lot of photo fragments. It’s like a puzzle, and requires a lot of going through the photos of other elephants that might show a part of the chosen one. This is the case of the State Elephant of Kolhapur. I only have fragments of different photos to show it to you, but none of the whole elephant. Researching I discovered a very admired and loved maharaja, that did wonderful great things for his people. He was the first Maharaja of Kolhapur and a great social reformer. He is highly remembered.

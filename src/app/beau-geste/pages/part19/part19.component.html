Chapter 19

The set that followed this time was another elephant. The State Elephant of Kapurthala. I chose this elephant because of the great photos that I had, and mainly because there was a small boy in the howda, probably the maharaja's son.

Beau Geste Set 320 - State Elephant of Kapurthala, Delhi Durbar, 1903.

The color combination on this elephant is one of my favorites, I think it looks very beautiful.

The Kapurthala elephant walking along Chandni Chawk street.

For the process of this elephant I began making my own stamps for the clothes patterns. The detail looked so neat, and it was easy to paint. The only thing I wasn't happy about is that the design is never a negative pattern, the embroidery always has a volume in positive on the cloth. Eventually I could correct that on future elephants.

Remember the last video that I posted? Well, my friend Bill enlightened me with his wisdom about its origin. The British Film Institute labeled the year incorrectly. It is in fact a film from 1912, when a pageant was held to honor the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to Calcutta. There were many elephants there too, as well as camel riders, flag bearers, horsemen and so much more. The filming equipment had been improved greatly by then probably, because the images are quite neater than in 1903. It is always such a pleasure to see the magnificent elephants walking by. I wanted to share that with you too. If you are able to enjoy it at least a fraction of the joy it gave me you will be very happy today. Enjoy!

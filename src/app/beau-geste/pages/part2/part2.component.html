Chapter 2

Right after the Alwar's carriage came the Alwar's Throne. Again, we only had Gertrude Bell's photos, and specially, her comments about which state the portraits were featuring. It is important to realize how difficult it was for the reporters, writers, journalists, painters and all of those who wanted to show what was happening during the event.
All the states of India traveled to the festivities, bringing almost everything they had with them. Soldiers, animals, jewels, everything. It took months to arrive at Delhi with such an amount of people and animals.
Each state had a camp assigned so they could stay before and during the event itself. So people trying to write or paint got overwhelmed by the amount of things to report. They could hardly keep track of names, titles and colours. You should add to that that many foreign countries were invited too! It was in this avalanche of information that Gertrude Bell labeled the Golden Tiger Throne as Alwar's. That's how we released it. But it turns it wasn't.

Later, when the research got more extensive, we found many pictures of the Retainers' Review featuring the Throne, and the comments on the program and the writers who wrote about it show the following:

Civil & Military Gazette 13/01:
'One of these litters was in shape a massive silver animal couchant and instantly resembling an insane tiger, with a silver chair on its back and a look of puzzled surprise in his eyes occasioned by finding that his tail was curved up to hold a silver umbrella.'

A Motley Army:
'After these revelations, you contemplated Rewa's great silver palanquin, fashioned like a lion, with comparative equanimity.'

Wheeler:
'The Rewa retainers escorted a dozen silver state litters, one of which was fashioned in the image of a tiger...'

So it turns out that the Alwar's Throne was in fact a silver palanquin belonging to Rewa, in the shape of a tiger or a lion. Or a monkey, as we called it. We didn't change the set's title because the set was retired by when we came to this knowledge. But I am glad that I can share this with you now.

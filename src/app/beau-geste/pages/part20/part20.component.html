Next came another elephant and its guards and band. It was the State Elephant of Faridkot. I chose it for two reasons. I loved the Maharaja's pose, sitting straight with his legs crossed, holding his sword very proudly. Also, the elephant's clothes were different to the rest. There was no embroidered pattern, simply lines and dots. A different elephant among all the richly decorated ones.

In this last part I want to explain a theory that came to me when I was making the State Elephant of Kolhapur, this new elephant that I have last released. This is just my theory, that cannot be easily confirmed. The programs don't mention this, the newspapers have no words about it, and none of the personal accounts that I've read speak about this at all.

When I am making a new elephant I pay special attention to the maharaja or the raja, since I think that they are the main character to be represented on the elephant, and their portrait is extremely important. When I began researching about the Maharaja of Kolhapur, Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj, I was surprised to discover that he was a very progressive and innovative ruler. He focused on helping the lower classes making education free for everybody, and giving chances to all work and study, even rural population and indigent people. He is highly admired, loved and remembered in Kolhapur.

Now, this was the only photo of the elephant that showed him best.

But when I researched about Shahu, his photos showed a different person.

Then it came to me. I had already DONE a portrait of this man on an elephant. He was very familiar to me. I immediately remembered the state elephant of Narsingharh. Doesn't this Maharaja look like Shahu?

Then I remembered that when I made the Narsingharh elephant (or the one I THOUGHT was Narsingharh), at the moment of researching the Raja, the only image that I came across was this one, of Arjun Singh:

Can he be the ruler in the howda of the new 'Kolhapur' elephant?

The State Elephant of Narsigharh came immediately behind the pair of Kolhapur-Rajgarh. What if the Kolhapur elephant got slightly delayed for some reason and Narsingharh went before, in its place, and not after as it was supposed to be? It could be possible. It's only that there is no mention of that, because the press only described the first six elephants of the Main Procession, and these were the 13th pair and the 14th, the one that went single.

This is just a theory that I will try to confirm with time. So for now, it's only a theory. What does this mean for the elephants displays you have at home? Nothing. The order is correct, only that the name of the state that identifies the elephants is different. Visually the disposition of the elephants is the same. You don't need to change anything. And if you agree with my theory, you only need to KNOW which state they represent.

I hope this hasn't confused you much. This is a theory, completely opened for discussion. I could well not have mentioned anything and leave things as they are, but it is not my style. I share what I study with you, and this was part of that. The elephants will continue be named as they were released, because I won't change any titles until I confirm this. But I thought you'd like to know.

If you have any knowledge or information that can confirm or refute this idea, it will be welcomed. I will be waiting for your comments on this (interesting and fun) ‘conundrum’.

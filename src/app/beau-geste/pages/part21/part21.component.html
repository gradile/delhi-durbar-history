Chapter 21

It was 2012, and the digital copies of the photographs that I bought from the British Library were more and more detailed and extremely interesting. One of the images portrayed a group from Kotah, with the State Elephant and three more Retainers' ones. I fell in love with one of them. We had already made a few elephants from the Retainers (the baby elephant, the executioner from Rewa, the first Narsingharh elephant, the Orchha elephant band) so I decided to make one of the Kotah ones. I was (and still am) very impressed with the detail of the photos and how it turned out.

Here is the original Kotah group from my digital copy

Beau Geste Set 346 - Elephant with Standard Bearers from Kotah, Retainers Review, Delhi Durbar, 1903.

After the new elephant I continued making more guards and musician groups for the elephants we had already made. I was very behind with those. Collectors were really hoping to complete their groups now that I had began focusing on the people around the elephants. So now it was Kapurthala and Bundi's turn.

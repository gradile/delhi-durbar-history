Chapter 22

Let's continue with the new chapter. It was time for a new elephant, and I chose Bikaner State. The Maharaja, General Sir Ganga Singh, was a very important character in India by then. He was modest and inconspicuous. His elephant didn't carry many jewels and his howda was very simple. He only wore his white uniform. I loved those details about him.

You surely know by now that I keep on studying my Delhi Durbar 1903 photos constantly. I am always discovering something new, finding details that I had missed. The detail in some of the photos makes it possible to enlarge the file and keep on finding new things. That is one of the reasons that the Prints Room at the British Library has a magnifying glass beside each of the albums requested.

So reviewing the photos, I found a stereoview portraying the Indore group outside the Amphitheatre, resting. I liked the group so much that I included them in my plans immediately.

Before continuing with the British Library report, I wanted to share a couple more videos that are very related with the elephants procession. Both are videos from the Mysore Dasara, a festival celebrated each year in Mysore from the end of September to October 4.

This first film shows the Mysore Dasara from 1968. The whole film is interesting, but you’ll notice that the film is repeated after the first half. A few things that I’d like share with you. From minutes 1:45 to 3:53 you can see the people decorating and painting the elephant, the jewels with which they are decorating the animal, and the way they are attached to the elephant. Later, from minutes 4:40 a 5:21 check the parade in front of the maharaja: state elephant, palanquins, howdas, dancing horses, carriages and even cars. The jewels are shown in great detail, one of the most interesting features of the film.

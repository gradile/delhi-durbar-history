Chapter 23

By this moment I knew that the elephants were becoming the most appreciated pieces of the Delhi Durbar line. Collectors were eagerly waiting for new releases, making space in their cabinets and getting more shelves to display them. So I planned them as to be released at least two new each year.
The following elephant was the State Elephant of Kotah. It walked 6th on the right column at the Main Procession. The ruler of the state was Maharajadhiraj Maharaja Mahim Mahendra Maharao Sir Umaid Singh II.

You know I always listen to your suggestions and wishes about groups to be made and released within the Durbar line. There are as many requests as collectors out there. Many of you had been asking for the Aides-de-Camps elephants at this point. Most collectors had the elephants of Lord Curzon and the Duke of Connaught released by Britains years before. And as these two elephants were preceded by the six elephants of the Aides de Camps of both authorities, these new group felt like it was a needed addition to the line. Making half of them didn't make much sense since they walked in pairs, and the six of them seemed excessive. So I went for the first four, leaving the rest two elephants for later.

Chapter 24

This is a special chapter for me, because I enjoyed so much making the following few sets that I will never forget them.
It became obvious by that time that we needed more spectators to add to all the parades. People were predominant during all the festivities. And no parade seemed realistic without them. The issue with people is that they are not wearing uniforms or state colors, so they had to be all different. That was a challenge at the time of making them, if I didn't want to make a master for each person figure on the set. So I designed them with different standard bodies, heads and arms, that I could combine to make them as different as they could. Added to that the different colors would help.
So I immersed myself in the study of 1900 clothes and fashion, both oriental and occidental. I simply LOVED the process. Luckily there is so much information about clothing and fashion out there, at hand. Modelling the clothes was very fun too. And so was making different hats and headdresses.
The sets were a huge success. We could hardly keep up with the orders. We added the possibility of ordering them in more different colors and poses for those who wanted more. I didn't want to release 15 different sets so the standard ones are only a few, but collectors know that we take special commissions regarding variations.
These are the standard released sets, and only a few selected images used to make them. I didn't want to flood the chapter with too many photos.

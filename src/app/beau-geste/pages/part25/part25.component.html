My next set to follow was a Sehr & Mokalla set. A wonderful photo from a stereoview showed a large group of flagbearers, and I thought that they'd go great with the infantry and band that we had already released a few years before. I luckily got a few photos of this Sehr & Mokalla group over the years. The camel riders, the large flag bearers, the spear bearers and, finally the elephant, all released as sets later. I hope I could have as many pictures of all the other states.

I keep on going through the photos I took at the British Library. I still have so much to go through. One of the photograph stamps showed a name that I hadn't researched before. Sidney Herbert Dagg. He was definitely present at the Delhi Durbar in 1903, and took photos for a few official albums.
Going through research results online I found a catalog selling a group of his photographs, sold by Justin Croft. Can you believe it? A bit expensive for me, but if any of you is planning to buy or already bought the group please, let me know

Here is a link to the catalog:
Justin Croft-Summer23-V2 Catalog.

Item number 14, in page 23, features the Dagg's album. One of the photographs shown on the sale lot is one that I had never seen. And believe me when I say that I have seen many many. This one surprised me for good. I wish I could ever see it in person. It was taken in front of the Town Hall, and features the first group of elephants of the Main Procession. Exquisite.

S.H. Dagg was the one who took the photos from a special Chandni Chawk spot, the one that shows the elephants returning from the Jumma Masjid, going towards the Town Hall. There were many photographs taken from there, hopefully portraying the whole procession, but only a few are known.

Dagg's spot on the Delhi Durbar route

Seeing a church behind the elephants, I searched through online maps to find which church could have been in Chandni Chawk in 1903. I found that the only one was the Central Baptist Church, built in 1814, so it was definitely there when the Durbar happened. Here are a couple of screen captures of the street and map nowadays.

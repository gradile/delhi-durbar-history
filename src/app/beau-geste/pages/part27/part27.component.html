Chapter 27

The following sets were 2 groups. One was an elephant and its escort guards and musicians, and the other was a cavalry group.
I mentioned that I had bought a digital image from Corbis, which was one of my first acquisitions. The State Elephant of Sikkim was clearly be seen there, and that was the first one we made from that photograph many years before. But there were many other elephants that were pictured so perfectly. My next choice was the State Elephant of Nabha. The Raja was such a magnificent character, he made me decide for his elephant to follow the line.

Hira Singh, Raja of Nabha in 1903.

The next group of figures were musicians on horse. The first one I discovered was shown in a Gertrude Bell's photograph, a drummer on horse dressed beautifully, with large feathers as ornaments. A delightful drawing by Raven Hill showed him as Datia's, so I went for the musician. Musicians, really. He was surely part of a band, and I made as many as I thought possible.
I love the colors on these sets.

Beau Geste Set 409 - Mounted Band from Datia, Kettledrum & Cymbals, Delhi Durbar, 1903.

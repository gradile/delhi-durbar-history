Chapter 29

Here I am with a new chapter of the history of the Delhi Durbar 1903 Beau Geste sets. By this moment I was already focused only on the Durbar, I was not releasing any sets from other periods. I know some of you were sad for that, but I simply cannot focus on many subjects at a time. The Delhi Durbar 1903, research and production, take most of my time. And it's my favorite subject. So got happily fully immersed in it by then.
The next group of sets was based on an astonishingly beautiful digital copy that I bought from the British Library. It was taken at the Amphitheatre on January 7, during the Retainers Review. It showed a splendorous flagbearer and a magnificent kettledrummer, both mounted, fully dressed with embroideries and silks. I chose them immediately. I found other photos of them among my files, and one of the photos identified them as from Nabha state. Years later this was confirmed, because I could identify the flagbearer on elephant that walked behind them as Nabha's as well.

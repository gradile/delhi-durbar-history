Chapter 33

Following the Rajgarh Elephant I went for a group of wonderful camels that I'd been wishing to make for a very long time. There are many many photos of them, they were mentioned in most reports about the Retainer's Review. Their colors and their standards were described in the books and newspapers. Here they are. The Emblem Carriers of Cutch.

About them:

Wheeler:
'Standard-bearers, mounted on camels, carried the banners of the rao of Cutch, one of which had been given to an ancestor by the Moghul Emperor Shah Alam, the other to the then Rao by Lord Lytton at the Imperial Assemblage in 1877.
The entire Cutch procession was, indeed, faithfully reproduced from a painting in the procession of the Rao, which depicts the ceremonies practiced by his ancestors.'

Madras Times, January 8:
'The most striking features were the flags and the emblems of Cutch carried on camels and elephants, and armed men on stilts.'

Printed Program, item 3:
'3) 12 Camels with musicians, standards and guns.'

Civil & Military Gazette, January 13:
'The camels carried two men each and were clothed in red trappings with swivel guns atop. The musicians blew penny trumpets, and big cudy horns and straight hugles like long stemmed funnels - all of silver.'

Menpes:
'Men on camels dressed in vermilion turbans carried salmon-and-gold banners surmounted by golden emblems. These banners and emblems played an important part all through the procession, and added their quota of colour and brilliance. They had been presented by the Mogul Emperors, our friend in salmon informed us, and acted as a stimulus to remind the various States that they formed part of the Mogul Empire.'

A short note to let you know that I'm in the middle of making the backdrop sets. They will be metal, thin, mainly flat, with some kind of relief. So many things to think about. I can't make them in the right scale since they were actually around 20 feet from the elephants, but the displays on the collectors shelves don't reflect the real scale. They are meant to be placed at the back of the elephants' parade and spectators, and they have to look as if they are at a distance. There will be trees too, and street lights. And more people.
So far, the drawings look awesome, and the sculptures are coming out beautifully. I don't have pictures to share now but I will have some final photos soon, probably by the end of July.

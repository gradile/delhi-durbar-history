Chapter 37

Continuing with the History of the Delhi Durbar Beau Geste sets, the releases that followed were two special ones. One was a large group that many collectors had been asking for for a long time, and the second was a couple of elephants that needed to complete a group that I had already begun a few years before.
The first group was a challenge to me: the Imperial Cadets Corps. They were a group of 27 horsemen, and I had to give some thought about how the sets would be released. I finally decided to present them as only one set, but announcing that the real group had 27 figures instead of the usual 3 that has a special horsemen set. The whole group had its lead in Sir Prataph Singh, in front, and then they were 20 cadets, 3 adjutants and 3 commanders. I introduced one of each of them to the collectors, and offered them any mixing choices coming in groups of three. The collectors with most space in their shelves went for all 27 figures, but other were a bit more limited and chose among a few sets of 3 figures each.
I present to you a photo of each of the different variables, all coded as Beau Geste Set 468.

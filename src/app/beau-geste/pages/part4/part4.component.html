Chapter 4

The next Delhi Durbar sets were the Jaisalmer ones. Our original Durbar collector/inspirator kept on providing photos of sets that could be made. He came across a photo of the Jaisalmer Palanquin and banners. As the Indian estates brought everything they could to the procession and the Retainers Review, the possibility of the palanquin actually being in Delhi then was very real. So we went ahead and made it.

This was the original photo

We released 2 sets from the pictures

Set 85 - Palanquin of the Maharaja of Jaisalmer, Delhi Durbar, 1903.

Set 86 - Servants with Ornamental Parasols, of Jaisalmer, Delhi Durbar, 1903.

Both sets have been retired long long ago. We also made the banner, but only as a special commission since it couldn't be released as a standard set because of the complexity of its making.

I could get a photo of the Maharaja of Jaisalmer much later, actually a Maharawal, Shalivahan Singh III. He didn't look like the one we chose for the set but that was all we could do in that moment, which such lack of information.

Our collector also had a photo of another palanquin that he wanted made. This one was a difficult one to make as a standard set since it required different materials (like the one that looks like glass) and a huge amount of work to be made, and the cost wasn't going to be appropriate for the market. We made a couple of them only. I believe it was Jhodpur's or Jind's. If any of you know for sure, please let me know.

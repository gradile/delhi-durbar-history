Now following with my Delhi Report. At the end of Queen's Road, the elephants turned right towards Elgin Road. That road goes all along the Red Fort west side. It was along this road that the Retainers elephants (around 250) waited standing on the sides for the Main Procession to pass in order to follow them.

The Retainers Elephants waiting for their turn to follow the parade, on the back of the photo, along the Red Fort. View from the South East corner of the Jumma Masjid. The spot where the fireworks happened can also be seen in the picture.

In 1903, the view from Queen's Road toward the Red Fort was clear, there was nothing in the way. Today, that sector has buildings in the way, so the only way to see the Fort would be from an aerial point of view.

Street that used to be Elgin Road.

I visited the Red Fort from the South. This is the ticket counter. You have to follow the road beside the Fort in opposite direction as the elephants in order to get to the entrance. This road is parallel to Elgin. People walk it every day in order to visit the Fort.

Walking from the South, there is empty land in the middle between this Fort road and Elgin (now Netaji Subash Marg). The elephants on the right side of Elgin stood right in this space.

The entrance to visit the Red Fort is just in front of the end of Chandni Chawk.

Inside the Red Fort. It was such a beautiful place to visit. You get to spend many hours there.

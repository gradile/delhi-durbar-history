Chapter 42

Following with the history line, we got to the next elephant and group. The State Elephant of Hill Tippera. This is a stunning elephant when you see it in person, because of all the gold and silver in its clothes, and the unique howda, very intricately ornated.
The Hill Tippera elephant walked in 18º place on the right column beside the State Elephant of Jind, behind Cooch Behar and Nabha, and in front of Rampur and Kapurthala. Hill Tippera was a North-East princely state during the period of the British Raj, and two years after it ended. Today is the Tripura State

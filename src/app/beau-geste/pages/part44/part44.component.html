Chapter 44

The following group of sets were from Cooch Behar.The State Elephant of Cooch Behar was in the Main Procession, and came 17th on the right column, just behind the State Elephants of Sikkim and Bahawalpur, beside the Nabha State Elephant and in front of the Hill Tippera and Jind ones.
These sets were released in May 2022.

Cooch Behar (Koch Bihar) was a Princely State of India during the British Raj. It was part of the Bengal States, located south of the Bhutan. The maharaja in 1903 was Nripendra Narayan.

The Cooch Behar elephant was included in the first photograph of the Delhi Durbar that I bought, from Corbis. It was a digital copy from the Sikkim elephant, but the photograph was so neat that all the elephants that followed were portrayed clearly. I used this image to make a few other elephants: Sikkim, Bahawalpur and Nabha.

I had been wanting to add more lining troops to the sets I had already made. These regiments were all along the Elephants' Route, and the two sets already released were not enough by far.
I went for one of the regiments that lined the parade along a section of Chandni Chawk, just in front of the Town Hall and the Clock Tower. I love how they complete the scene.

Beau Geste Set 502 - 20th Brownlow's Punjabis, Lining Troops, Delhi Durbar, 1903.

Pictures of the Making Of.

---------------------------------------------------------

Now, back to the Delhi Chapter on the Elephants' Route. They finished their surrounding of the Jumma Masjid on the North-East corner. From there they took Esplanade Road to the left, towards Chandni Chawk. There are no photographs (that I could find) taken from this stretch. Either there weren't photographers along this path or not many bystanders? I wish someone could have taken their camera. With less people around, it was a great opportunity to take photographs closer to the elephants.

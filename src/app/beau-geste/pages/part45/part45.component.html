By September 2022, the new group of sets were from Sirmur State. The Sirmur Elephant was at the Main Procession, walking in 20th place on the left column, beside the State Elephant of Benares, behind Kapurthala's and Rampur's, and in front of Banda's and Tehri's.
The Raja was Sir Surendra Bikram Prakash Bahadur. The state was located North, within the Punjab states. It was founded in 1616.

The Procession Route. We got to a spot that was, and still is, a bit uncertain for me. It is a spot from where a few photographs were taken, but I don't know if there was a stand, a platform or something where the photographers could have stood. I got a couple of pictures from this spot many years ago, and I assumed that they were taken from Queen's road. But as years passed and I payed more and more attention to it, I arrived at the conclussion that they were taken on Chandni Chawk, just after the elephants turned left from Esplanade Road. This made more sense, since the street is a boulevard, and the elephants would be walking on the right direction.
Red arrows show the spots I have already featured. The blue arrows show the section, and the direction from where the photographs were taken. The star is a church, that was always on the background of all the pictures from this location. It doesn't show in the map form 1903. But checking the modern Delhi map I found a church in the exact spot where it was photographed in 1903. It is the Central Baptist Church, built in 1814. I took a couple of pictures during my last trip to Delhi, included below. If this church was there, if it looked like in the picture, or if it is indeed that one or not, I don't know at all.

Chapter 49

A new chapter of the History of the Beau Geste Delhi Durbar 1903 sets. A group of sets from Tonk, that were really popular. Also, one of the last chapters of the Delhi main route. I'm already planning more chapters about the Delhi Durbar, regarding more details about all the events that took place in 1903. I have so much information about it, and I'd love to share it.
The next group was released April 2024. It was the State Elephant of Tonk, and its guards and musicians. I also could add some soldiers to the group. I was very lucky to find a picture of a few Tonk soldiers in one of the 1903 Durbar albums at the British Library. A blurry picture, that was more than enough. And the Alwar's Carriage can be seen in the background!!! It was taken outside the Amphitheatre during the Retainers Review, on January 7, when all the states were settled in the surroundings, waiting for their turn to enter the parade.
Tonk was a Indian princely state under the British Raj. It was located mainly in the actual state of Rajastan. The Nawab in 1903 was Muhammad Ibrahim Ali Khan. The State Elephant of Tonk was the 10th on the right column of the Main Procession, just behind Alwar (and Dewas Senior), at the side of Dewas Junior and in front of Sirohi (and Samthar).

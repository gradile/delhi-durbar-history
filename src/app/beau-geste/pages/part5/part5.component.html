Chapter 5

It was 2002 then and it was time for our first State Elephant. As we still only relied upon Gertrude Bell's photographs, we had only one picture of this elephant with the proper identification of the state so we went for it.

This was the only photo we had to be used as a model:

Beau Geste Set 87 - The State Elephant of Patiala, Delhi Durbar, 1903.

With the years, I learned so much more about this elephant and its state. The Maharaja, Bhupinder Singh, was 12 years old at the time of the 1903 Delhi Durbar, so the maharaja sitting inside the howdah should be a young boy instead of the man that we made for this elephant. As we got to find more detailed photos it became clear that our set needed more detail to be accurate. I will probably have to make a new Elephant of Patiala when I finish all the Main Procession and some elephants from the Retainers Review that I'd like to make too. We'll see.

Bhupinder Singh, Maharaja of Patiala, 1903.

Most of the photos of the State Elephant of Patiala that I could gather were mainly from postcards or stereoviews that a great friend shared with me, or digital copies I bought from the photo albums from the British Library.

Here are the photos, during the Main Procession, during the Retainers Review or posing before it or at their camp.

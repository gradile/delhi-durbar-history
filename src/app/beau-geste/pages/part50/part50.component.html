This is a special chapter of the Delhi Durbar. As you know, the Retainers Review was held at the Amphitheatre on January 7, 1903. This is my favorite activity of the Durbar. All the States that were invited paraded around the arena with all they had: elephants, men, bands, camels, horses and any other animals. During the Chiefs Review, the people could admire each state in full. The maharajas and rajas were sitting at the tribune, watching their states go by. It must have been such a special occasion for those who were there.
Here is a map where you can see where the Amphitheatre was. Quite North from the city, surrounded by all the camps of the different states. The circle marks the spot, and the black line below shows where the Main Procession took place.

After my visit to Old Delhi following the Elephants Route, my taxi took me to the place where the Amphitheatre used to be. Nowadays it is called Coronation Park. Almost nothing from 1903 remains there. It is a very peaceful field were you can spend your hours enjoying silence (after the noise of the city).

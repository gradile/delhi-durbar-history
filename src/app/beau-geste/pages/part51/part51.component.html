In April 2025 we released the Sirohi group, and a new group of scenography sets. The State Elephant of Sirohi with its group. This elephant walked 11th on the right column of the Main Procession, side by side with the Samthar elephant, and in front of Jhalawar and Charkkhari. Before Sirohi were Tonk and Dewas Junior.

The ruler was HH Rajeshwar Maharajadhiraj Maharao Shri Sir Kesri Singhji Bahadur (1857–1925), who ruled Sirohi from 1875 to 1920. The Kingdom of Sirohi, later known as Sirohi State during the colonial period, was founded around 1311 CE by the Deora branch of the Chauhan Rajputs. Located in present-day Rajasthan, it evolved from a Rajput kingdom into a princely state under British paramountcy. Following Indian independence, Sirohi was integrated into the Indian Union between 1949 and 1950, and its territory was later incorporated into Rajasthan.

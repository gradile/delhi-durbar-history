Chapter 6

We still didn't have more information than Gertrude Bell's photos, so she was mainly leading the way of the sets. The following were The Kashmir (or Kashmiri) Giants. They were only two. Most of the information I could get was that they were brothers, and that they were from Kashmir. But one of the digital copies that I bought from the British Library names them as Abdul Razag and Lur Khan, 7 feet 10 and 7 feet 8, one of them from Kashmir and the other from Secunderabad.

When we planned the set we decided to include Mrs Bell as well. We felt that we owed her so much for all we could get from her photographs that she deserved to be in the set as well.

Beau Geste set 94 - Horns and Drums Courtship, Delhi Durbar, 1903.

As we were advancing in our research we learnt that there were movies taken from the Main Procession. A great friend got the first copies from the British Library and shared them with me. I still remember the huge excitement of seeing the elephants walking, in action, after seeing so many photos. I watched the short clips many many times. With the years, the movies began to be shared, and they ended all in You Tube, uploaded by the British Film Institute in the best quality that the original films allowed.

Here is one of the films taken from the most usual spot for the photographers. On the stairs of the Jumma Masjid, looking at the elephants coming from the Red Fort along Khas Road.

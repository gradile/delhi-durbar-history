Chapter 8

I can't help but think that the history of the Beau Geste sets is going to have many chapters. I become aware of that with each new chapter. There are SO many sets in the Delhi Durbar line and I honestly can't estimate how long this will take. Not that I am not happy writing it. But still, so many more chapters to go. We got to year 2003. My son Julian was 16, and Daniel invited him to help us with our line. He was born to me painting miniatures and his father sculpting, so he's never known different. Daniel assisted him to making his first set, the Cutch spear bearers. Julian's always been a very talented sculptor, since he was very young, so he excelled in this task. His lancers were very well received, and he was very happy with the feedback. And mom couldn't be prouder

Julian at the Beau Geste table during the London show in 2004.

His brand-new set was based on the following photo, taken from Wheeler's book photo featuring the Cutch contingent:

Beau Geste Set 113 – Cutch Spear Bearers, Delhi Durbar 1903.

With the years we got a photo with better definition, but it's always been the only photo that we’ve had.
----------------------------
The following set was one of the most loved and collected sets in the history of the Delhi Durbar line.
The Main Procession had many elephants in it. The first elephants were the most relevant states, thought to be 50 at the beginning, but two of them couldn't make it for several reasons. The Maharaja of Baroda knew about the death (or illness, I am not sure) of his wife by that day, and he missed the Main Procession. And the elephant of Cutch got a bit mad before the procession and it could not be controlled, so the Maharaja of Cutch could not make it either. Both contingents were present in the Retainers Review and the second column of elephants after the main procession. Only both maharajas missed the December 29th opening of the events.

After the Main Procession came the Retainers elephants. They were all the elephants each state had brought, and they were… 250!!!
Leading the Rewa contingent came a baby elephant, we immediately decided to make it.

Here is the only photo that we could count on by then (exact definition and size).

Beau Geste Set 114 – Baby Elephant from Rewa, Delhi Durbar, 1903.

I got many more photos with the years. It was something such a lovely view. The photographers focused of the baby elephant, luckily.

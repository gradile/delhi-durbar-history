Continuing with the Delhi Durbar films, I came across this one about a month ago and was absolutely delighted by it. Especially with the system they used to run it, and with the idea that there might be more of those out there. It was a Kinora picture reel.
This 1903 Delhi Durbar film is short, but full of details. You'll see there how much the howdahs on the elephants moved, shaking the people in them. Notice the fly whisks carried by the state elephant of Dewas Senior, and how the servants shook them up and down.
The Maharaja of Alwar sat down during this part of the trip. The elephant necklaces moved from side to side, probably with a clinking sound along with the bells, jingle bells and the rattles. The ornamental clothes hanging from the elephants’ ears danced from side to side.
At the back right, you see the elephants coming out from Khas Road, and turn left to go around de Jumma Masjid. As I said, such a delicious piece of history.

"The Kinora was the first home movie entertainment system and was invented by Lumière in France in 1896. The rights were later purchased by the British Mutoscope & Biograph Company.
The Kinora is known as a flipper viewer. The filmed sequence of photographic images was attached to a reel which could then be rotated by a crank handle, animating the images. Kinora reels ran for approximately 30 seconds and could be purchased or rented for home use. Reels depicting family members could also be made at photographic studios, and from 1908 a camera was available for people to purchase to make their own Kinora home movies."
NFSA National Film and Sound Archive of Australia. https://www.nfsa.gov.au/latest/magical-kinora

"Basically, a miniature version of the mutoscope for home use, the Kinora worked very much like a flip book in the shape of a Rolodex. It used conventional monochrome photographic prints fixed to strong, flexible cards attached to a circular core. A reel revolved handle by turning a handle, making the pictures flip over against a static peg one by one. The moving pictures could be viewed through an eyepiece."
